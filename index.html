Yun-Ting Cheng

Hello, I'm Yun-Ting Cheng (鄭昀庭), a postdoctoral researcher at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) and California Institute of Technology (Caltech), working with Olivier Doré and Tzu-Ching Chang. I completed my Ph.D. in the Caltech Observational Cosmology group under the supervision of Jamie Bock.

My research focuses on extracting cosmological and astrophysical information from large-scale structure surveys. A large part of my work focuses "intensity mapping," which maps the large-scale structure using the entire emission field instead of resolved sources as in conventional redshift surveys. I am interested in optimizing the extraction of desired information from current and future cosmological surveys (such as SPHEREx, Roman, Euclid, LSST, etc.). This involves two primary areas: first, developing novel analysis algorithms to constrain information from intensity mapping datasets, and second, constructing data-informed models to improve cosmological analysis.

My work spans various topics, including the development of data-driven algorithms for intensity mapping data analysis, pipeline construction and data analysis for the CIBER experiment to constrain the intra-halo light signal in the extragalactic background light, as well as the development of foreground mitigation algorithms for line intensity mapping surveys.
